Power Industry Professions

Energy is the fundamental element that underlies all human activities, both social and economic, and is essential for powering our daily lives. The generation of power is necessary for every stage of the production and consumption process, from creating goods and transporting them to storing and providing support services. Essentially, energy is integral to all aspects of the economy and society.

Biofuel Engineer

Biofuel engineers are engineers that help us reduce our carbon footprint by finding uses for fuel that is made from plant sources. Biofuel engineers ensure that the biofuel is generated efficiently to be used for electricity, heating homes, and powering vehicles. They do this by designing devices, procedures, and processes.

Career details:

Like all engineering careers, biofuel engineers are problem solvers. They spend their day analyzing existing biofuel systems so that they can work on improving them or designing new ones. Leadership skills are very important for a person in this career because they may oversee other engineers involved in biofuel production.

What is biofuel energy?

This is a type of energy that is made from organic matter.

Skills required for this career:

  • Leadership skills
  • Excellent problem-solving skills
  • Research skills
  • Critical thinking skills

Study options:

  • B.Eng. Chemical Engineering
  • Bachelor’s in Mechanical Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Agricultural and energy companies
  • Biofuel organizations
  • Renewable energy companies
  • Universities
  • Research laboratories

Chemical Engineer

Chemical engineers play an important role in supporting research and development in the manufacturing, healthcare to biotechnology fields.

Career details

Chemical engineers are professionals who improve equipment used in the processing of chemicals and they find solutions to the problems many industries face. They have developed solutions for diseases, cleaning water, generating energy, and much more.

These professionals rely vastly on their knowledge of mathematics and science. They are not only involved in making things, but their knowledge is also applied in areas like law, publishing, and medicine.

Skills required for this career:

  • Exceptional problem-solving skills
  • Excellent verbal and written communication
  • IT and numeracy skills
  • Analytical skills

Study options:

  • B.Sc. Engineering
  • B(Eng) Chemical Engineering
  • National Diploma Chemical Engineering
  • B.Sc. Chemical Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Chemical manufactures
  • Council for mineral technology
  • Government departments and universities
  • Organizations such as CSIR, ESKOM, AECI, Sasol, AEC, Sentrachem

Electrical Engineer

Electrical engineers develop electrical products and systems, manage engineering projects, perform quality performance and analysis on new systems, and much more.

Career details:

An electrical engineer is involved in the design and implementation of electrical systems. This job demands electrical engineers to have thorough analysis skills. Electrical engineers have a wide range of opportunities available to them, these individuals are needed in almost all areas of product development such as robotics, space, and more.

The life of an electrical engineer has a lot of day-to-day responsibilities such as making sure that the products are of the best quality by testing them. They develop manufacturing processes, and they provide support for electrical work.

Skills required for this career:

  • Attention to detail
  • Project management skills
  • Strong organizational and communication skills
  • Analytical skills

Study options:

  • Bachelor Electrical Engineering/li>
  • Bachelor Electronic Engineering
  • B.Sc. Information Engineering
  • B.Sc. Electrical Engineering

    Possible employers:

  • Manufacturing companies
  • Telecommunications companies
  • Research and development companies

Electromechanical Engineer

Electromechanical engineers possess disciplines from both mechanical and chemical engineering. They are responsible for building devices that blend electrical and mechanical components from computers, and equipment used for gas systems or radar systems.

Career details:

These professionals often work with other engineers to determine the project’s scope and then design and build systems that the customer wants.

Employers usually prefer candidates with a few years of experience working within electrical and mechanical systems. To be successful in this career you need to have a strong mathematical and physics background and a love for challenges.

Currently, in South Africa, the University of Cape town is the only tertiary that offers the electromechanical engineering degree course. Although mechatronic engineering is a similar field and is offered at many South African universities.

Skills required for this career:

  • Good problem-solving skills
  • Excellent teamwork skills
  • Strong analytical skills
  • Attention to detail

Study options:

  • B.Sc. Mechanical Engineering
  • B. Eng. Mechatronic Engineering
  • Bachelor in Mechatronic and Mechanical Engineering
  • Bachelor in Mechatronics

Possible employers:

  • Government departments and municipalities
  • The mining industry
  • Manufactures of electrical equipment
  • Private engineering consultants

Energy Efficiency Technician

Energy efficiency technicians monitor and maintain the systems that provide us with power, heat, and water. They evaluate electrical installations and processes to determine the amount of energy they use, and they implement ways to improve energy consumption.

Career details:

For energy efficiency technicians to do their jobs effectively they must be able to read schematics and interpret blueprints. These individuals have a wide range of responsibilities. They repair and replace motors or transformers. They also test and troubleshoot electrical equipment to find and fix malfunctions.

Energy efficiency technicians work in a variety of settings, and they may be exposed to hazardous materials. Organizations hire energy efficiency technicians to help them reduce their carbon footprint and energy consumption.

Skills required for this career:

  • Technical skills
  • Mechanical aptitude
  • Attention to detail
  • Good problem-solving skills

Study options:

  • B.Eng. Engineering
  • B.Sc. Electrical Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Power Plants
  • Factories
  • Government department
  • Private consultancies

Geophysicist

Geophysicists use various methods like gravity, magnetic, electrical, and seismic to study the physical aspects of the earth. They collect data on seismic waves, for oil and gas companies to know what lies below the earth’s surface.

Career details:

They collect data on seismic waves, for oil and gas companies to know what lies below the earth’s surface.

What are seismic waves?

Seismic waves are caused by the sudden movement of materials within the earth.

Geophysicists are also responsible for examining the physical properties of rocks and gathering and evaluating well data to build reservoir models.

When you go into this career your daily duties might also include, pre-plan projects before going on-site, interpreting map 2D and 3D seismic data, and more There are three areas that geophysicists usually work in, solid earth, fluid earth, and upper atmosphere.

Skills required for this career:

  • Numerical skills
  • Project management skills
  • Analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Critical thinking skills

Study options:

  • B.Sc. Geological Science
  • B.Sc. Geography
  • Bachelor Environmental Health

Possible employers:

  • Mining and construction companies
  • Mapping service companies
  • Instrument manufacture and development firms
  • Public Utilities

Geothermal Technician

Geothermal technicians work at locations where geothermal energy is harnessed for use. A geothermal technician’s main role is to monitor the process that leads to electricity and heat production and step in when adjustments and repairs are required.

Career details

These technicians test, install, and maintain geothermal equipment and ensure that safety protocols are being followed. On a typical day, geothermal technicians identify and correct malfunctions in geothermal plant equipment. You will also find these technicians operate equipment such as excavators, pavement saws, ground mixers, or rock hammers. As part of their responsibilities, they also must adjust power production systems to meet load and distribution demands.

Geothermal technicians do not need a degree to get into this role but obtaining an engineering-related degree is helpful when the technicians want a higher salary.

What is geothermal energy?

It is energy created in the earth in the form of heat.

Skills required for this career:

  • Analytical and critical thinking skills
  • Good problem-solving skills
  • Attention to detail
  • Good verbal and written communication skills

Study options:

  • B.Eng. Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Geothermal energy plants

Hydro Power Plant Controller

Hydropower plant controllers work in a hydropower plant as technical support who installs, operates, and maintains the hydro power plant processes, systems, facilities, and equipment.

Career details

Hydropower plant controllers clean and maintain the equipment to prevent failure. They operate the plant equipment like the turbines, pumps, valves, gates, fans, or battery banks. As part of their duties, they operate high-voltage switches in hydropower stations.

A hydropower plant controller supervises and controls the application of resources, operations, and activities within the hydropower plant.

Skills required for this career:

  • Problem-solving and troubleshooting skills
  • Technical knowledge
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills
  • Ability to work in a team

Study options:

  • B.Sc. Industrial Engineering
  • B.Eng. Electrical Engineering
  • B.Eng. Mechanical Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Hydroelectric power stations

Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Engineers

Hydrogen fuel cell engineers design, develop, construct, modify, and test fuel cells that convert fuel into electricity. They track the building of prototypes and test and adjust their products so that the products perform properly.

Career details

Daily, hydrogen fuel cell engineers may perform analysis using electrochemical or methodological diagnosis. This role requires an extensive amount of thinking and analyzing and being a creative thinker is a very important skill for this career. Employees in this career are responsible for a wide range of duties such as validating the designs of fuel cells. They write technical reports and they authorize the release of fuel cell parts for production. This type of engineer is also involved in planning and conducting experiments to validate new materials, optimize start-up protocols, reduce conditioning times, or examine contaminant tolerance.

What is a hydrogen fuel cell?

Hydrogen fuel cells are used to power cars, buses, or trains as a clean alternative to petrol or diesel.

Skills required for this career:

  • Excellent verbal and written communication
  • Strong problem-solving and troubleshooting skills
  • Detailed-orientated and analytical
  • Critical thinking skills

Study options:

  • B.Sc. Mechanical Engineering
  • B.Sc. Chemical Engineering
  • B.Sc. Electrical Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Industrial plants
  • Laboratories

Nuclear Power Plant Controller

Nuclear power plant controllers are responsible for the safe operations of nuclear reactors. They have several responsibilities, monitoring equipment that is used to detect radiation levels. They maintain and repair equipment used in the nuclear power plant.

Career details:

They inspect equipment to ensure that it is not malfunctioning and they perform tests on the equipment to ensure they perform properly.

Working in this career can be stressful as you must be able to make quick and safe decisions in emergencies. They implement procedures that regulate the start-up or shutdown of the facility.

These controllers, control the nuclear reactor, adjust control rods, monitor the reactors and respond to abnormalities, and repair them.

Skills required for this career:

  • Technical skills
  • Good verbal and written communication skills
  • Analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Ability to work in a team

Study options:

  • Advanced Diploma in Electrical Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Nuclear power generation plants

Power Generation Operations Manager

BPower generation operations managers are individuals who ensure safety protocols are being followed at the power plant so that the power plant is operating smoothly.

Career details:

Power generation operations managers have a wide range of responsibilities, they identify opportunities to reduce energy consumption, and they coordinate power plant shutdowns and startups. As power generation operations managers, they check that all the equipment in the facility is up to date and performing well. As part of their responsibilities, they maintain a cost-effective production process by using only the resources they need to achieve production targets. And they also isolate the waste and other constraints that limit productivity.

Skills required for this career:

  • Leadership skills
  • Technical knowledge
  • Ability to work in a team
  • Excellent problem-solving skills

Study options:

  • B.Sc. Science
  • B.Sc. Industrial Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Power plants

Renewable Energy Engineer

Renewable energy engineers work to minimize the negative impact on the environment by working on the production of renewable energy.

Career details:

They research and develop equipment to help harness energy from sustainable sources such as wind, or solar.

Renewable energy engineers are involved in a wide range of duties such as designing solar panels and conducting research to develop new technologies.

They develop plans to help organizations transition to alternative energy sources. Renewable energy engineers work in many different types of settings, they work in industrial plants, and wind or solar farms. These engineers oversee the entire installation of renewable energy systems.

Skills required for this career:

  • Project management skills
  • Excellent problem-solving skills
  • Mathematics and scientific ability
  • Ability to work in a team

Study options:

  • B.Sc. Physical Science
  • B.Eng. Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Energy management companies
  • Factories
  • Waste management companies

Solar Power Plant Technician

These technicians assemble, install, and maintain solar panels on homes, organizations, or open land. Their job is to make sure that the solar panel equipment that they install works properly, generating as much electricity as possible.

Career details:

Solar power plant technicians work with different types of equipment such as photovoltaic cells, inverters, or tracking systems.

Solar power plant technicians are responsible for many different tasks, such as performing routine maintenance on roofs. Helping to design and plan new solar panel systems, and they also repair and maintain these solar panel systems.

These technicians work indoors and outdoors and may be exposed to all kinds of weather conditions. They can work on rooftops or the ground depending on which solar panel they are installing.

Skills required for this career:

  • Strong communication skills
  • Good problem-solving skills
  • Ability to work independently
  • Ability to read blueprints

Study options:

  • B.Eng. Sustainable Systems and Energy
  • B.Eng. Sustainable Energy and Environmental Engineering
  • B.Eng. Building Services and Renewable Energy

Possible employers:

  • Public power utilities
  • Private solar power companies

Wind Energy Engineer

Wind energy engineers are engineers who design wind farms, and they harness the power of the wind. They are also involved in supervising the manufacture of rotor blades or other equipment.

Career details:

For wind energy engineers to design wind farms they develop site specifications, plan to manufacture, and test all the hardware and electrical components. They even go as far as to build the roads and transport the components to installation sites.

Some wind energy engineers analyze the aerodynamic properties of new designs, run tests, and document the results. These engineers may also do tasks like evaluating issues of compliance with health and safety codes.

Skills required for this career:

  • Good verbal and written communication skills
  • Ability to work in a team
  • Critical thinking skills
  • Complex problem-solving skills

Study options:

  • B.Sc. Mechanical Engineering
  • B.Eng. Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Industrial plants
  • Laboratories
