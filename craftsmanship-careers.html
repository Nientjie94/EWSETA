craftsmanship Careers:

Artisans are competent and highly talented individuals who primarily work with their hands. Trades and artisan careers are in high demand, and artisans are paid well! It is no longer a strict requirement to have a university degree to become proficient in your career.

Electrician

Electricians are people that work on electrical equipment for homes or businesses. They are mainly responsible for installing and repairing complex electrical control or wiring. They work with other electricians and maintenance workers to install or repair electrical systems.

Career details:

Throughout the day electricians travel to different sites to perform electrical tasks such as installations, repairing, or light fixtures.

Electricians perform a variety of tasks, they diagnose electrical problems and plan the layout and installation of electrical wiring, equipment, and fixtures.

Skills required for this career:

  • Strong work ethic
  • Exceptional communication and customer service skills
  • Strong problem-solving skills
  • Critical thinking skills

Study options:

  • Bachelor of Science
  • B.Sc. Electrical engineering

Possible employers:

  • Factories
  • Construction companies
  • Businesses
  • Homes

Biomass Plant Technician

A biomass plant technician works at a biomass plant and maintains the equipment. These technicians operate equipment such as boilers, generators, and gasification equipment. They are responsible for many other duties such as inspecting the equipment to ensure that they are functioning properly.

Career details:

They spend a lot of time analyzing and repairing equipment, which makes the role a very physical and technical one. But their most important mission is to ensure the production of renewable energy is done so safely by monitoring and controlling the equipment.

To be a biomass technician a college degree is not necessary to have but, you must be mechanically skilled as an employee.

What is a biomass plant?

A biomass power plant produces electricity by burning natural materials such as wood, waste, and sewage or plants.

Skills required for this career:

  • Excellent problem-solving skills
  • Good written and verbal communication skills
  • Excellent Mechanical skills
  • Analytical thinking skills

Study options:

  • Bachelor Electrical Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Renewable energy companies

Plumber

Plumbers are well-trained professionals who install and repair plumbing systems in residential and commercial properties. Plumbers design and inspect plumbing systems, perform diagnostics and ensure compliance with regulatory codes.

Career details:

Successful plumbers know how to work alone without supervision and fix problems quickly and efficiently. Daily, plumbers perform duties that include reading and interpreting blueprints, fitting and repairing household appliances, and cutting, welding, and assembling pipes. And plumbers have excellent knowledge of water supply, heating, and ventilation systems.

In South Africa, you do not need a college degree to become a plumber, but industry-recognized training is required to become a qualified plumber. And you must also pass the trade test to become a qualified plumber and then you are allowed to register with the plumbing industry registration board.

Skills required for this career:

  • Good interpersonal skills and patience
  • Excellent problem-solving skills
  • Mechanical skills
  • Safety skills

Study options:

  • National Certificate: Plumbing N1-N3 Health

    Possible employers:

  • Public Utilities
  • Government departments
  • Shipbuilding or aircraft construction companies
  • Self-employment
  • Businesses

Welder

A welder is responsible for repairing damaged pieces of metal or assembling pieces of metal using heavy machinery that releases heat, and that heat melts the metal into place.

Career details:

A welder’s responsibilities may vary according to the industry they are working in. Some of their duties may include, reviewing blueprints, cutting metal, smoothing melted metal to remove creases, and sourcing the material that will be used in the project.

They may also help set up scaffolding to reach high places.

Skills required for this career:

  • Excellent communication skillss
  • Knowledge of metallurgy
  • Attention to detail
  • Being able to work well in a team

Study options:

  • B.Eng. Engineering
  • B.Sc. Engineering
  • Welding: N1

Possible employers:

  • Manufacturing plants
  • Constructions companies
  • Mining companies
  • Government departments
  • Shipbuilding
  • Railroad manufacturing
  • Industrial maintenance and repairs

Mechaninical Engineer

Mechanical engineering is a very broad field, and you will find that people in this profession work in a variety of different types of industries and products.

Career details:

Mechanical engineers design, develop, build, test, and inspect mechanical devices and systems, such as machines, tools, engines, heating and cooling systems, transportation systems, and robotics. These individuals are creative thinkers who are responsible for several things such as producing outline designs, testing, and evaluating theoretical designs, and they produce effective solutions to emerging problems.

To be a successful mechanical engineer, you must be a highly creative individual who can come up with innovative solutions to problems. Mechanical engineers usually work in offices where the computer plays a major role. People that choose this career have options to specialize in areas such as transportation equipment, fluid mechanics, or even construction.

Skills required for this career:

  • Creativity
  • Problem-solving skills
  • Mathematics skills
  • Clear communication abilities

Study options:

  • Bachelor Mechatronics
  • Bachelor Mechanical Engineering
  • B.eng. Mechanical Engineering ENGAGE
  • B.Sc. Mechanical Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Manufacturing industries
  • Government departments
  • Universities
  • Mining companies
  • Consulting engineering firms

Laboratory Analysts

Laboratory analysts use sophisticated equipment to analyze samples taken from animals, plants, humans, and other sources. Lab analysts make use of various techniques to perform various types of tests.

Career details:

Being a laboratory analyst means that you will be performing duties like preparing solutions for testing, sterilizing equipment, preparing reports, and analyzing chemical samples.

Lab analysis work in a variety of settings, either alone or with other scientists and technicians. These individuals must wear protective clothing and adhere to safety procedures because they are exposed to hazardous materials.

Skills required for this career:

  • Attention to detail
  • Communication skills
  • Critical thinking skills
  • Organization skills

Study options:

  • B.Sc. Chemistry
  • B.Sc. Biological Sciences
  • Bachelor Applied Science

Possible employers:

  • Medical Labs
  • Forensic Labs
  • Health care companies
  • Manufacturing companies
  • Law enforcement

Geologist​

A career in geology is the study of the earth, its history, resources, and how the natural environment is affected by the earth’s materials. On a day-to-day basis, geologists plan geology projects, gather and analyze geological data, and study the earth’s crust and various materials.

Career details

If you choose a career in this field, don’t be afraid to get your hands dirty because you will also be collecting samples and specimens from sites of interest. At times you will be working on mines to determine the location and the grade of the ore and ensure the ore is mined to maximize the profitable extraction of the ore deposit.

Geologists are involved in several responsibilities such as writing reports on findings, coordinating research programs, and study sites for developments, formations, hazards, and composition.

Skills required for this career:

  • Good analytical mind
  • Attention to detail
  • Great oral and written communication
  • Organizational and teamwork ability

Study options:

  • B.Sc. Geological Science
  • B.Sc. Geoinformatics
  • B.Sc. Geospatial Science
  • Bachelor Geomatics
  • B.Sc. Land Surveying

Possible employers:

  • Universities
  • Petroleum and mining companies
  • Government departments
  • Chamber of mines
  • Civil engineering firms

Fitter and Turner

In this career, you will not only be responsible for maintaining and fixing machinery. But you are also responsible for studying the blueprints, plans, and drawings to be able to construct assemble, manufacture and fit parts of machines.

Career details

Fitter and turner professionals spend a lot of their time bending, standing, and crouching. Their work environment can be noisy, crowded, or dirty.

These are highly skilled individuals that are high in demand. They work in many sectors of the economy, and they use their skills to maintain and repair different types of large machinery such as diesel-powered generators, machinery in processing plants, parts of trains and railway tracks, and more.

Skills required for this career:

  • Communication and active listening skills
  • Analytical and quality control skills
  • Physical strength and fitness
  • Organizational skills

Study options:

  • National Certificate: Fitter and Turner (N1 – N3)
  • National Certificate: Mechanical Engineering

Possible employers:

  • Engineering companies
  • Government departments
  • Mines
  • Iron and steel plants
  • Large industrial plants and factories

Boilermaker

A boilermaker is an individual who constructs, assembles, and installs boilers.

Career details

A boilermaker is responsible for several things such as, casting and shaping pieces, testing complete boilers, and upgrading boilers to match environmental standards. When you are a boiler maker you are responsible for reading blueprints for location, position, dimensions, and more.

What are boilers?

Boilers are closed vessels where water and other liquids are heated, and steam or vapor is generated. Boilers are used for steam radiators, and radiant floor systems, or they are used to heat the hair via a coil. The boilers are also used in factories and ships.

Skills required for this career:

  • Proficient in welding skills
  • Critical thinking skills
  • Reading comprehension
  • Good Physical strength

Study options:

  • National Certificate: Boilermaker N1-N3
  • Boiler Maker Engineering N1-N6
  • Boiler Making Level 1 – 4

Possible employers:

  • Building and construction companies
  • Shipbuilding
  • Manufacturing companies
  • The rail industries
  • Oil and gas companies
  • Industrial plants
